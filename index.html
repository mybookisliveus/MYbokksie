A Glimpse of the Land and Its People

The United States is a large and diverse country, with everything from coastlines and deserts to forests and mountains. Most people live in cities, but many communities have strong historical ties to the land. The economy is driven by technology, services, education, and industry.

To understand American history, it's helpful to look at its main themes: the history of its Indigenous peoples, the colonial period, the country's founding principles, the effects of wars and economic changes, the growth of a multicultural society, and the ongoing work toward equality.

Highlights in history

Ancient History

For thousands of years, Indigenous peoples built complex societies and managed the land.

Colonial Beginnings

Early colonies developed their own governments, which laid the groundwork for a new country.

Westward Movement

In the 1800s, westward expansion brought new people and led to major social changes and growth.

A New Nation

The Declaration of Independence and the Constitution created a new country based on new ideas.

Major Challenges

The Civil War, two World Wars, and the Great Depression changed the country's society, economy, and place in the world.

The Push for Civil Rights

Key events in the Civil Rights Movement show the continuing fight for justice and changes in the law.

The Importance of Studying the Past

Studying history helps us understand where our modern government and society came from, why we still debate certain issues, and how we can improve in the future. It shows how communities have handled economic problems, how cities developed, and how different groups of people created a shared identity. It also helps us learn from parts of history that have often been ignored.
